🎯 Objective

Beat the dealer by building a hand whose total is closer to 21 than the dealer's — without going over (busting). You are not competing against other players, only against the dealer.

A Blackjack (an Ace + any 10-value card on the initial deal) beats all other hands and typically pays 3:2.

🃏 Card Values

2 – 10

Worth their face value. A 7 is worth 7, a 3 is worth 3, and so on.

J, Q, K

Face cards are each worth 10, regardless of suit.

Ace

Worth 1 or 11 — whichever benefits your hand more. An Ace counted as 11 makes the hand "soft".

Suits

Suits (♠ ♥ ♦ ♣) have no effect on hand value in standard blackjack.

▶ How a Hand is Played

  1. Place your bet before any cards are dealt.
  2. You and the dealer each receive two cards. Both of your cards are face up; the dealer has one face up and one face down (the hole card).
  3. You decide how to play your hand using the available actions.
  4. Once you finish, the dealer flips their hole card and plays by fixed rules — no choice involved.
  5. Hands are compared and bets are settled.

⚖ Player Actions

Hit

Take another card from the deck. You can hit as many times as you like, but if your total exceeds 21 you bust and lose the bet immediately.

Stand

Keep your current hand and take no more cards. Play passes to the dealer.

Double Down

Double your bet and receive exactly one more card. A powerful move when the odds are in your favor.

Split

If your two starting cards share the same rank, split them into two separate hands, each with its own bet. Split Aces receive only one additional card each.

🆕 Dealer Rules

  • The dealer must hit on any total of 16 or below.
  • The dealer must hit on a soft 17 (a 17 containing an Ace counted as 11). This is the H17 rule used by Agent B21.
  • The dealer must stand on hard 17 or higher.
  • The dealer has no choice — these rules are fixed and automatic.

💰 Payouts

  • Blackjack — Pays 3:2 (win 1.5× your bet)
  • Regular win — Pays 1:1 (win 1× your bet)
  • Push (tie) — Your bet is returned, no win or loss
  • Bust — You immediately lose your bet when exceeding 21
  • Dealer Blackjack — All player hands lose (unless the player also has Blackjack — a push)

💡 Tips to Get Started

  • Always assume the dealer's hole card is a 10-value card — it's the most common value in the deck.
  • Never bust when the dealer shows a weak card (2–6). Let the dealer draw into a bust instead.
  • Always split Aces and 8s. Never split 10s or 5s.
  • Double down on 11 against most dealer upcards.
